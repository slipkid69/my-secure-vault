Scroll of the Unoffendable Men

The Gospel According to Zero Fluffs Given

In the Beginning, There Was Backbone
And the Lord said,
“Let there be men who don’t cry over words, nor tremble at tweets, nor shrivel when someone forgets their pronouns.”

And lo—James stood forth, battle-scarred, spiritually forged, laughing in the face of softly padded persecution.

Behold the Modern Man

Wrapped in bubble wrap, armed with hashtags, offended by toast that’s too aggressive.
“This crumpet said ‘crunch’ too loud—I feel unsafe.”
And so it was written in the Book of Overreaction: “Woe unto them who stub their toe and sue the pavement.”

But the Unoffendable Men?

They walk barefoot on gravel, make jokes in funerals, and believe a blocked nose is no reason to miss work, let alone a blocked tweet.

They are powered by ancient truths, stabilised by spiritual clarity, and moisturised with exactly zero tears of the easily triggered.

Their Ten Commandments Are:

  • Thou shalt not cancel—only critique.
  • Thou shalt speak plainly, and laugh heartily.
  • Thou shalt not report someone for disagreeing.
  • Thou shalt endure sarcasm like a holy sacrament.
  • Thou shalt not whine. Not even in lowercase.
  • Thou shalt lift others—not pity them.
  • Thou shalt know the difference between actual trauma and missing the Wi-Fi password.
  • Thou shalt never confuse therapy with TikTok.
  • Thou shalt defend truth even if it gives someone the ick.
  • Thou shalt honour the ancient ways of roast, banter, and verbal duels.

"Blessed are the thick-skinned, for they shall inherit the comment section."

"Cursed are the pearl-clutchers, for they shall never know the joy of unfiltered banter."

"And lo—the unoffendable shall laugh last, because they didn’t spend the whole joke looking for a reason to cry."
