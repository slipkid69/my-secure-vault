⟁ 3.1: What Is Gnosticism?
Gnosticism (from the Greek gnosis, meaning “knowledge”) emerged in the first and second centuries A.D., masquerading as a spiritual philosophy of liberation. But it was not a variation of Christianity—it was a counterfeit gospel.
At its core was a claim:
• That the true God is hidden
• That the God of the Bible is an evil lesser being called Yaldabaoth (also called Samael, “the blind god”)
• And that secret knowledge (gnosis) would free the soul from this prison of flesh
In Gnosticism, the Serpent was the hero.
In Eden, he gave knowledge, not sin.
The Cross was a trap, not salvation.
And Jesus was not the Son of God, but an awakened spiritual messenger who came to remind humans of their hidden divinity.

⟁ 3.2: Key Gnostic Systems
• Valentinians (2nd c. AD): Created complex cosmologies of divine emanations (Aeons) from the “Monad” (unknown god). Sophia’s fall led to the creation of the material world.
• Sethians: Taught that Seth (son of Adam) was the father of a divine bloodline, placed in the world by the true God to resist the demiurge.
• Mandaeans: Still active today in Iraq/Iran, they revere John the Baptist, reject Jesus as a false messiah, and believe the soul must ascend through barriers of archons (rulers).
• Cathars & Bogomils: Medieval Gnostics in Europe who believed matter was evil, the cross a symbol of Satan, and the church a deception. Thousands were executed during the Albigensian Crusade.
All taught a common thread:
That salvation comes through knowledge, not repentance.
That Jesus was a guide, not a Saviour.
That the body is a trap, and God’s Law a prison.

⟁ 3.3: The Gnostic Legacy Today
Though the early church rightly declared Gnosticism heresy (see Irenaeus, Against Heresies), its core ideas persist today in:
• New Age movements
• Theosophy (Blavatsky)
• Mystery schools & secret societies
• Pseudoscientific spirituality (e.g., “Christ consciousness,” “divine spark,” and “ascension”)
• Even in self-help culture, where the self is treated as the source of salvation
The Gnostic spirit always offers an alternative light—but it is a light without a cross, and a god without repentance.

The Gospel of Thomas, Judas, and Other Forgeries

Gnosticism produced numerous false gospels, many written centuries after Christ:

  • Gospel of Thomas – A collection of sayings, some cryptic, some heretical
  • Gospel of Judas – Portrays Judas as a hero helping Jesus escape the flesh
  • Gospel of Mary – Presents Mary Magdalene as the supreme revealer of hidden truth

These writings were rejected by the early church because they:

  • Contradicted the known teachings of Jesus
  • Appeared centuries later
  • Promoted elitism and secrecy

The Serpent Rebranded

The Ophites (Ophidians) – Worshippers of the Serpent

Among the most brazen of the Gnostic sects were the Ophites, whose very name comes from the Greek “ophis”, meaning “serpent.”

Where others merely hinted, the Ophites glorified the serpent of Eden. They believed the serpent was the true liberator, and Jehovah—the God of the Bible—was a blind, jealous demiurge seeking to keep mankind in ignorance.

Core Ophite Beliefs:

  • The serpent was the bringer of gnōsis (knowledge) and thus a saviour
  • Jehovah (YHWH) was not the Most High, but a lesser, evil being
  • Jesus was a messenger from the “real” unknowable god to awaken divine spark within mankind
  • The Archons (rulers), including biblical angels, were prison wardens keeping souls trapped in flesh
  • Salvation meant escaping this material world and ascending through secret knowledge past the Archons

Ophite Rituals and Symbolism:

  • Used serpent imagery extensively in their symbols and teachings
  • Some accounts report rituals involving serpent veneration, even the use of serpents in communion rites
  • Gospels such as the Gospel of Eve and Gospel of the Serpent (now lost) are attributed to their teachings

“Woe to those who call the serpent holy…” — (Isaiah 5:20, paraphrased)

Their belief system is the purest inversion of Genesis—what God called cursed, they called divine.

Why the Ophites Matter Today

  • New Age serpentine symbolism—kundalini energy, chakras, enlightenment spirals
  • Luciferian ideologies—embracing the light-bringer archetype as freedom, not rebellion
  • Esoteric occultism—which venerates forbidden wisdom and often hides serpent motifs in plain sight

Even the medical symbol of the caduceus (a winged staff with two entwined serpents) is an ancient pagan emblem, not the biblical serpent of healing from Numbers 21. The Ophites reveled in such confusion.
